# Smart City Complaint Analysis

## Problem Statement

Modern cities receive thousands of citizen complaints related to garbage collection, water supply, road damage, street light failures, traffic congestion, drainage problems, and public safety issues. Monitoring and resolving these complaints efficiently becomes difficult as cities grow. This project aims to analyze complaint data, identify recurring issues, evaluate service performance, and provide actionable insights to improve urban services and citizen satisfaction.

---

## Dataset Description

Dataset Used: 311 Service Requests Dataset

The dataset contains citizen complaints submitted to city authorities. It includes information such as complaint category, complaint date, resolution date, department, location, complaint status, and other service-related details.

**Note:** Due to GitHub file size limitations, only a sample dataset containing 10,000 records has been included in this repository. The complete dataset is available from the original source.

## KPI Definitions

The following Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) were created:

- Total Complaints
- Resolved Complaints
- Resolution Rate
- Average Resolution Time
- High Priority Complaints
- Citizen Satisfaction Score
- Complaint Hotspot Zones
- Department Performance Score

These KPIs help city authorities monitor operational efficiency and service quality.

## Key Findings

- Certain complaint categories occurred more frequently than others.
- Some city zones experienced repeated civic issues.
- Resolution time varied significantly across departments.
- High-priority complaints generally required longer resolution periods.
- Complaint trends fluctuated across different months and periods.
- Service performance varied depending on complaint type and department workload.

---

## Business Questions Answered

### 1. Which complaint categories occur most frequently?

The analysis identified the most common complaint categories reported by citizens, helping authorities focus on major service concerns.

### 2. Which areas experience recurring civic problems?

Several zones consistently recorded higher complaint volumes, indicating recurring infrastructure and public service issues.

### 3. Which departments take the longest to resolve complaints?

Department-wise analysis revealed differences in resolution efficiency and highlighted departments requiring process improvements.

### 4. What factors reduce citizen satisfaction?

Long complaint resolution times, repeated complaints, and delayed responses to critical issues were major factors affecting citizen satisfaction.

### 5. What actions should city authorities prioritize?

Authorities should prioritize high-complaint zones, improve department response times, and focus on recurring infrastructure problems.
